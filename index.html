Personal Profile

I hail from rural Vermont and am a senior at Olin College in Massachusetts, with a major in engineering and a concentration in computer science, and have been doing computer science as well as robotics research since freshman year. I have spent my summers gaining industry experience, and for the last two, I’ve been at Onshape in Cambridge, Massachusetts, a well-funded new start-up which intends to reinvent CAD.

Above all, I like to learn. Until I got to college, I was largely self-taught. I really appreciate a good design and the expression of an artistic sensibility. I consider myself alert to the world and interested in new things. Lately, I’ve learned to bake bread and have taken up photography. Pottery is a long-standing interest. In everything I do, I strive for simplicity and elegance.

After Olin, my plan is to pursue a Ph.D, but I hope to have some memorable adventure along the way.

DynaRoACH Controls

In connection with my research for the ORB Lab at Olin College, dynaRoACH is a cardboard hexapod robot driven by single motor. Neural Network

This is a feed forward neural network, created for fun, capable of training on a GPU (using Cuda) and CPU (using Armadillo for fast matrix math). It allows an arbitrary number of layers. It trains using mini batch gradient decent with momentum. This network can be used to classify MNIST, (hand written digits) with greater than 95% accuracy.
